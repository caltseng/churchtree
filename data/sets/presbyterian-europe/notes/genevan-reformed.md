# Genevan Reformed (Calvin)

## Summary
- The Genevan Reformation shifted Geneva from Roman Catholic rule to Protestant self-rule and Reformed theology under John Calvin, making Geneva a major hub of the Reformation.

## Key Dates
- start_year: 1536
- end_year: unknown
- Ecclesiastical Ordinances: 1541
- Servetus execution: 1553
- Academy of Geneva founded: 1559
- Calvin's Geneva: 1536-1564

## Relationships
- Parent/precursor bodies: Roman Catholic rule under a prince-bishop allied with Savoy.
- Split or merge relationships: Geneva adopted Reformed theology and church governance under Calvin; Geneva influenced Reformed churches across Europe.

## Distinctives
- Reformed theology (Calvinism) and disciplined church governance.
- Genevan Consistory established for church discipline.
- Geneva served as a training and sending center for Reformed pastors.

- Estimated size (if known): unknown

## Events
- 1541: Ecclesiastical Ordinances establish church governance framework.
- 1553: Execution of Michael Servetus.
- 1559: Founding of the Academy of Geneva.
