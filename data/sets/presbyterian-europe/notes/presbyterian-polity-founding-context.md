# Founding Context of Presbyterian Polity

## Summary
- Francis Makemie, ordained in the Presbytery of Laggan in Ireland, is widely regarded as the founder of American Presbyterianism and organizer of the first presbytery in America (Philadelphia, 1706).

## Key Dates
- start_year: 1706
- end_year: unknown
- Makemie ordination (Presbytery of Laggan): 1681
- Presbytery of Philadelphia organized: 1706
- Makemie trial before Lord Cornbury: 1707

## Relationships
- Parent/precursor bodies: Presbytery of Laggan (Ulster, Ireland) ordained Makemie.
- Split or merge relationships: Formation of the Presbytery of Philadelphia as the first organized presbytery in America.

## Distinctives
- Presbyterian polity of graded courts (session, presbytery, synod, general assembly).
- Emphasis on elder-led governance in early American Presbyterianism.

## Size / Scale
- Estimated size (if known): unknown

## Events
- 1706: Presbytery of Philadelphia organized; Makemie chosen as moderator.
- 1707: Makemie tried for preaching without a license, highlighting early religious liberty conflicts.
