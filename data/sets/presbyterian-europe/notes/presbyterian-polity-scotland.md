# Presbyterian Polity Emergence (Scotland)

## Summary
- Presbyterian polity is governance by assemblies of elders, with graded courts of session, presbytery, synod, and general assembly; in Scotland, presbyteries were formally created in 1581.

## Key Dates
- start_year: 1581
- end_year: unknown
- Creation of original presbyteries: April 1581
- Abolition of synods in Church of Scotland: 1993

## Relationships
- Parent/precursor bodies: Rejection of episcopal governance by bishops.
- Split or merge relationships: Presbyterian polity became the standard structure for the Church of Scotland and influenced Reformed churches elsewhere.

## Distinctives
- Rule by elders and graded courts (session, presbytery, synod, general assembly).
- Ordained ministers conduct services.

- Estimated size (if known): unknown

- 1581: Creation of original presbyteries in the Church of Scotland.
- 1993: Synod level abolished in the Church of Scotland.
