# Scottish Reformation / Church of Scotland

## Summary
- The Scottish Reformation broke with Roman Catholicism and established the Protestant Church of Scotland, adopting a Presbyterian structure and largely Calvinist doctrine under John Knox and the Lords of the Congregation.

## Key Dates
- start_year: 1560
- end_year: unknown
- Reformation Parliament: 1560
- Formal ratification by James VI: 1567

## Relationships
- Parent/precursor bodies: Roman Catholic Church in Scotland.
- Split or merge relationships: Establishment of the Protestant Church of Scotland as a national Reformed church with Presbyterian structure.

## Distinctives
- Presbyterian polity and Calvinist doctrine.
- National church closely tied to Scottish political developments.

## Size / Scale
- Estimated size (if known): approx 245,000 members (2024)

## Events
- 1560: Reformation Parliament adopts Protestant creed and rejects papal supremacy.
- 1567: Ratification of Reformation legislation by James VI.
