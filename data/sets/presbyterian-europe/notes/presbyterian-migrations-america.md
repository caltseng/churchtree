# Presbyterian Migrations to Colonial America

## Summary
- Presbyterianism arrived in the American colonies in the early 1700s; in 1706 seven ministers organized the Presbytery of Philadelphia, the first presbytery in what became the United States.

## Key Dates
- start_year: 1683
- end_year: unknown
- Synod of Philadelphia created: 1717
- Old Side-New Side division: 1741-1758

## Relationships
- Parent/precursor bodies: Colonial Presbyterian congregations operating independently before 1706.
- Split or merge relationships: Formation of the Presbytery of Philadelphia and later synods and presbyteries established a colonial Presbyterian structure.

## Distinctives
- Early congregational autonomy before presbytery formation.
- Synod and presbyteries provided oversight, discipline, and ordination.

## Size / Scale
- Estimated size (if known): over 200,000 Scotch-Irish migrants to the Americas (1717-1775)

## Events
- 1683: First known Presbyterian church in America organized in Maryland.
- 1706: Presbytery of Philadelphia organized; Francis Makemie chosen as moderator.
- 1717: Synod of Philadelphia created with three presbyteries.
- 1741-1758: Old Side-New Side controversy and reunion.
